# NZ Vehicle Theft Analysis 🚗

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes stolen vehicle data in New Zealand over six months, sourced from the New Zealand Police Department’s Vehicle of Interest database. Each record represents a stolen vehicle and includes details on vehicle type, make, year, color, date stolen, and region.  
The goal is to uncover trends in vehicle theft and provide insights and recommendations for owners, law enforcement, and communities.

## 📊 Key Questions
- Which vehicles are stolen most often?  
- What vehicle ages are targeted?  
- Which days of the week and months see the highest theft rates?  
- Which regions experience the most vehicle thefts?  
- What vehicle colors and types are most at risk?

## 📈 Findings

### Vehicle Age
- Vehicles aged **11–20 years** account for almost half of all stolen cars.  

### Vehicle Type
- **Stationwagons, saloons, and hatchbacks** are stolen most often.  

### Vehicle Colour
- **Silver, white, and black vehicles** are targeted most frequently.  

### Day of the Week
- **Mondays and Tuesdays** record the highest theft rates.  

### Month of the Year
- **March** has the highest theft rate, while **April** has the lowest.  

### Community & Regions
- Some regions experience **higher theft volumes** than others.  

---

## 📊 Findings & Recommendations

### Vehicle Age
- **Finding:** Vehicles 11–20 years old are stolen the most.  
- **Recommendation:** Owners should install alarms, steering locks, or GPS trackers. Insurance companies could offer discounts for protected vehicles.

### Vehicle Type
- **Finding:** Stationwagons, saloons, and hatchbacks are most stolen.  
- **Recommendation:** Police should focus patrols on these vehicles. Owners should park in secure, monitored areas.

### Vehicle Colour
- **Finding:** Silver, white, and black vehicles are targeted most often.  
- **Recommendation:** Avoid dark streets and use visible deterrents like wheel clamps or alarms.

### Day of the Week
- **Finding:** Mondays and Tuesdays are the peak days for theft.  
- **Recommendation:** Owners should be alert at the start of the week. Police should increase patrols on these days.

### Month of the Year
- **Finding:** March sees the highest thefts; April the lowest.  
- **Recommendation:** Increase awareness campaigns in February and March. Communities should stay alert during high-risk months.

### Community & Regions
- **Finding:** Some regions have more thefts than others.  
- **Recommendation:** Police should analyze hotspots, allocate more resources, and strengthen community watch programs.
